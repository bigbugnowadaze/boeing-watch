WHAT THIS IS
AND WHO RUNS IT

Boeing Watch is an independent accountability dashboard for The Boeing Company. It is operated by one person, in public, under their own name, against a documented methodology, with a standing corrections policy. It carries no advertising, no analytics tracking, no sponsorships, and no financial relationship of any kind with Boeing, its suppliers, the Federal Aviation Administration, or any party regulated by the FAA.

THE MISSION
§ 01

The Boeing Company manufactures aircraft that carry more than two and a half million people per day. Its conduct is a matter of public concern. Its safety record is a matter of public record. Its regulators are a matter of public record. The financial press tracks its stock; the trade press tracks its production figures; the wire services track its press releases. Until now, no single English-language site has tracked the in-service difficulties of its airframes against a fixed methodology and a fixed corrections policy.

Boeing Watch exists to do exactly that. The mission is narrow and stated three ways so that it cannot be quietly broadened:

  • To publish, on a fixed cadence, the public-record events that bear on the question of whether Boeing aircraft are being designed, manufactured, and maintained at a standard consistent with the company's stated commitments and its regulatory obligations.
  • To do so against a methodology that is itself published, dated, and versioned — so that a reader, a regulator, a lawyer, or a journalist may inspect not only the numbers but the rules that produced them.
  • To correct mistakes promptly, in public, with the original wording preserved — so that the site's honesty over time may be audited by anyone who chooses to.

Boeing Watch does not seek to bring Boeing down, to lobby for any particular regulatory outcome, to short its stock, or to recruit a constituency against the company. It is not an advocacy organisation. It is an instrument of record.

HOW THIS SITE IS PUT TOGETHER
§ 04

The front page is a static HTML document served from Cloudflare's global edge. It carries no third-party tracking, no advertising network, no analytics SDK, and no social-platform pixels. Aggregate, privacy-preserving traffic statistics are collected via Cloudflare Web Analytics, which does not set cookies and does not identify individual visitors.

Live data — the count of 737 MAX aircraft in the sky, the current BA share price, the latest Service Difficulty Reports — is pulled from named upstream sources at the cadences described in Methodology. When an upstream source is unreachable, the front page falls back to the most recent snapshot embedded in the page and switches the topbar indicator from LIVE to SNAPSHOT. No live counter on this site ever invents a value.

Routine writing is performed by a small set of named automated agents, each with a published prompt, a documented schedule, and a single narrowly-defined job. The prompts are stored in /agent-prompts in the source repository and are versioned alongside the methodology. The agents are:

SDR Beat Reporter
Normalizes raw FAA Service Difficulty Reports into the canonical sentence stem. Runs hourly. Model: Claude Haiku 4.5.
Diarist
Writes the daily diary entry from the previous twenty-four hours of records. Runs at 04:00 UTC. Model: Claude Sonnet 4.6.
Anomaly Editor
Detects statistical departures from the thirty-day baseline by aircraft type and JASC code. Runs nightly. No language model; statistics only.
FOIA Clerk
Drafts FOIA request letters from detected anomalies for human approval. Never auto-sends. Editorial gate is the editor's review.
Fact-Checker
Weekly audit of the past seven days of published entries against the current methodology. Flags inconsistencies for correction.
Wire Operator
Posts new severe events to Bluesky, Mastodon, and Threads on a sixty-minute delay. Template-based; no language model in the loop.
Compositor
Renders the previous day's diary, map, and SDR list as a four-page PDF and publishes it to the wire archive.

Editorial responsibility for every line published on this site, automated or not, rests with the named editor. The agents are tools. They are not authors.

THE FOUR CREDIBILITY CONSTANTS
§ 05

This site is built around four practices that, in the editor's reading, distinguish work that survives legal pressure from work that does not. They are stated here as a public commitment.

  • A named, accountable author. See §02.
  • A published methodology. See /methodology.
  • A standing corrections policy. See /corrections.
  • Sourced citations on every numeric claim. See /sources.

If any of these four conditions ceases to hold, the affected pages will be unpublished until the condition is restored. The four are not aspirational. They are operating preconditions.
